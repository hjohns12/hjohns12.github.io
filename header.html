I'm Hope Johnson.

I am a data scientist at the Princeton Gerrymandering Project. I am on the team developing OpenPrecincts, a database of precinct and electoral data to help citizens participate fully in redistricting. I'm also using statistics to investigate racial and partisan gerrymandering around the country.

I previously did statistics and data science at Mathematica Policy Research. At MPR I described, analyzed, and simulated public health data.

In the past, I have worked on projects related to police accountability, digital privacy, and prison abolition.

I was an undergraduate at Macalester College where I received degrees in statistics and economics.

I live in Brooklyn, New York.
